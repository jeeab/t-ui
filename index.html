Install it on your T‑Deck

Your browser can't flash devices — please use Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge on a computer. Flashing needs to run over a secure (https) connection.
1Plug the T‑Deck into your computer with a USB‑C cable
2Put it in flashing mode: hold the trackball pressed down while you flip the power switch on
3Click Install above, pick your device from the port list, then Connect
4Wait for the bar to finish — done!
💡 The device must be in flashing mode or it won't show up in the port list — hold the trackball down, then turn the power switch on, and keep holding until it's on.
  • Works on Windows or Mac — use Chrome or Edge (no driver install needed).
  • For the standard LilyGo T‑Deck (not the e‑ink “Plus/Max”).
  • A USB‑C data cable (some cables are charge‑only).
  • A microSD card in the T‑Deck for Maps, Notes, saved pins, and add‑on apps.
  • A GPS module is recommended so everything works — it's what puts you on the map and sets the clock. Not required: the rest of the device works fine without one.
⚠️ Installing erases the device and sets it up fresh (any existing Meshtastic identity/channels are wiped). That's normal for a first install.

📌 First things to know

  • The screen locks when it sleeps. The default unlock code is 1234 — change it in Settings → Lock PIN.
  • Taps landing in the wrong spot? Press Alt + C on the keyboard to re‑run screen calibration. Works even on the lock screen.
  • Double‑click the trackball to wake the screen and to go Home from anywhere.
  • The first start sits on the Meshtastic logo for 15–20 seconds — that's normal, don't unplug it.
What is T‑UI?

T‑UI is a friendly home‑screen “launcher” for the T‑Deck, built on top of the open‑source Meshtastic mesh‑radio firmware. Your T‑Deck still does everything Meshtastic does (off‑grid text messaging over long‑range radio, no cell service needed) — T‑UI just wraps it in a phone‑like grid of apps and adds a bunch of its own.

Everything is baked into the one file you flash above — all the apps below come with it.

How to use your T‑Deck

Getting around

  • Tap the touchscreen to open apps and press buttons.
  • Double‑click the trackball to go Home from anywhere, and to wake the screen.
  • Swipe the home screen sideways for a second page of apps. Long‑press a tile to rearrange.
  • Taps landing off target? Press Alt + C to re‑run screen calibration (works on the lock screen too).

The lock

  • The device locks itself when the screen sleeps, and asks for a code. The default code is 1234.
  • Set your own in Settings → Lock PIN → Change. Double‑click the trackball on the Home screen to lock it on purpose.

The apps

  • Mesh — the full Meshtastic messaging/nodes/map.
  • Maps — your own offline map with drop‑pins (see the Maps guide below).
  • Files — browse the SD card and internal memory; make folders, copy, trash.
  • Notes, Calculator, Stopwatch & Timer, Flashlight, plus games (Snake, Breakout) and a Metronome.
  • Share files (Wi‑Fi) — in Settings; sends files to/from your computer wirelessly (turn it on only when you need it).

Transferring files (the Share screen)

  • Open Settings → Share files → Open. It shows an address like ftp://192.168.1.42 and a login (tdeck / 1234).
  • On your computer, use a free FTP app like FileZilla (Host = that IP, Username tdeck, Password 1234, Port 21). Windows Explorer's built‑in FTP is unreliable — FileZilla works best. Keep the Share screen open while transferring.
Getting maps for your area

Map images aren't included — they'd be enormous, and everyone wants a different part of the world. There are two ways to get them, and the first one needs nothing but the device itself.

📲 Download them on the T‑Deck (easiest)

  • Open Maps, tap the gear, and choose Download this area.
  • Frame the area you want on the map first — what you can see is what it downloads.
  • Pick a detail level and it shows you the size before you commit. Each level is roughly four times the tiles of the one below, so start modest.
  • It uses Wi‑Fi, picks up where it left off if interrupted, and the screen can sleep while it works. Stay on the download screen though — leaving it stops the download.

Two map sources are built in, both free and happy to serve large downloads:

  • USGS Topo — the United States. Public‑domain topographic maps from the US Geological Survey.
  • TopPlusOpen — Europe‑wide (and usable worldwide at lower detail), published by Germany's federal mapping agency.

Pick your source from the same gear menu before downloading.

Map credits. USGS Topo is from The National Map, a work of the US Geological Survey and in the public domain. TopPlusOpen is © Bundesamt für Kartographie und Geodäsie (BKG), provided as open data under dl‑de/by‑2‑0. These credits are also shown on the device itself whenever the maps are on screen.

💻 Load them from a computer (anywhere else in the world)

If you're outside the regions above — or you want a different style of map — the classic method still works, and T‑UI uses the same map format as Meshtastic, so any T‑Deck map guide applies.

  • Maps are standard OpenStreetMap tiles — small .png squares organised by zoom level.
  • They go in a folder named /maps at the top level of the SD card, in /maps/<zoom>/<x>/<y>.png form.
  • Use a community “map tile downloader” for the Meshtastic T‑Deck to grab your area (pick a region and zoom range, it produces the /maps folder), then copy that folder onto the SD card.
  • Pop the card back in — the Maps app now shows your area, and you can long‑press to drop pins.

Tip: search “Meshtastic T‑Deck offline map tiles” for the current downloader tools and step‑by‑step. Start with a smaller zoom range so it doesn't take forever.

Adding a Meshtastic channel

A channel is a group you talk on. Everyone on it shares one key, so only people you've given it to can read the messages. Out of the box you're on LongFast — the public channel almost everyone uses. You can add private ones alongside it (a family group, say) without losing LongFast.

📱 From the Meshtastic phone app (easiest, and the most reliable)

  • Install the Meshtastic app on your phone, then pair with the T‑Deck over Bluetooth. The pairing code appears on the T‑Deck's screen.
  • Go to Channels. You can add, rename and delete channels straight from there, and it takes effect on the device immediately.
  • To share a channel with someone, use the share / QR button. That gives you a QR code and a link — they scan or open it and they're on.

Tip: a channel with a blank name is LongFast. A channel actually named “LongFast” is a different channel that nobody else is on — so leave the name empty if you're trying to be on the public one.

💾 From a file on the SD card (no phone needed)

Handy when you want to hand the same channel to several devices, or you don't want to pair a phone each time.

  1. Get the channel link — in the phone app, Channels → share → copy link. It looks like https://meshtastic.org/e/#…
  2. Save that link into a plain text file called channel.txt at the top level of the T‑Deck's SD card. Just the link, nothing else.
  3. On the T‑Deck: Settings → Add channel → Read card.
  4. It says how many channels it added. Restart the device to finish.

Adding is additive — your existing channels stay. A channel whose name matches one you already have is updated rather than duplicated, and your region is never changed by a shared link.

🔑 A channel link contains the encryption key. Anyone who gets it can read that channel. Treat it like a password — fine to text to family, not fine to post publicly or screenshot into a group chat.
Make your own apps

You can write your own little apps and games for the T‑Deck in a simple language called Lua — just a text file, no programming tools needed. Even better: hand the app‑maker's guide to an AI assistant and describe the game you want.

  • An app is one file called main.lua, placed in /apps/<yourapp>/ on the SD card. It shows up as a tile automatically.
  • Apps draw text and boxes, play beeps, and respond to taps — full details (screen size, commands, a working example, and a copy‑paste prompt for an AI) are in the app‑maker's guide.
